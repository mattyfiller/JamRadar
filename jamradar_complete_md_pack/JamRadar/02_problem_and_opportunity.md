# 02 - Problem and Opportunity

## Problem

People miss action-sport events because there is no single reliable place to find them.

A skier or snowboarder may need to check:

- Multiple mountain websites
- Instagram accounts
- Local ski shops
- Park crew pages
- Brand pages
- Community groups
- Event pages
- Friends' stories

This creates a bad experience:

- Users miss rail jams.
- Users miss registration deadlines.
- Users do not know which events are beginner-friendly.
- Users get too many irrelevant posts on social media.
- Mountains struggle to reach the right audience.
- Smaller events do not get enough visibility.

## Opportunity

JamRadar can become the trusted event and alert system for action-sport communities.

The opportunity is not just listing events. The real opportunity is:

- Personalization
- Radius-based alerts
- Event-specific filters
- Registration reminders
- Local discovery
- Organizer tools
- Gear/shop integrations
- Marketplace features

## Why Instagram Is Not Enough

Instagram is useful for hype, but weak for planning.

Problems with Instagram:

- Posts disappear in feeds.
- Algorithms hide important information.
- Users must follow many pages.
- Event details are often buried in captions.
- There is no clean map or calendar.
- Notification controls are poor.
- Registration reminders are not built in.

JamRadar should use Instagram as inspiration for energy and community, but not copy Instagram as a social feed.

## The User Pain in One Sentence

“I want to know about cool events near me without stalking every mountain, shop, and local account.”

## The Organizer Pain in One Sentence

“I want to reach the right riders without depending only on Instagram and hoping people see the post.”

## Market Entry Wedge

Start with:

- Ski and snowboard rail jams
- Terrain park events
- Indoor freestyle training
- Gear swaps
- Local mountain events

Then expand into:

- Skate
- MTB
- BMX
- Wake
- Surf-adjacent training
- General action-sport community events
