# 01 - Overall Concept

## Working Name

**JamRadar**

Other possible names:

- SendMap
- DropList
- ParkSignal
- Local Send
- EventLift
- RailRadar
- SnowPing
- SpotDrop

## One-Sentence Pitch

JamRadar is an event discovery and notification app for ski, snowboard, skate, and action-sport communities, helping users find nearby rail jams, park events, mountain events, indoor training sessions, gear deals, and local meetups without needing to follow dozens of scattered Instagram pages.

## The Core Idea

Users choose:

- Their location or travel radius
- Sports they care about
- Specific event types
- Notification preferences
- Favorite resorts, parks, shops, and facilities

Organizers post:

- Events
- Registration links
- Date changes
- Prize details
- Categories
- Photos and videos
- Sponsor details
- Gear deals
- Training sessions

The app becomes a centralized radar for local action-sport culture.

## The Main Pain Point

Right now, event information is spread across:

- Resort websites
- Resort Instagram pages
- Local park crew accounts
- Snowboard/ski shop pages
- Club pages
- Eventbrite
- Posters in lodges
- Group chats
- Word of mouth
- Brand sponsor pages

Because of this, users miss events, registration deadlines, date changes, and local opportunities.

## The Product Promise

**Never miss the events you actually care about.**

JamRadar should not become another noisy social app. The value is filtered, location-based, interest-based discovery.

## Primary Event Categories

- Rail jams
- Terrain park events
- Ski competitions
- Snowboard competitions
- Banked slaloms
- Demo days
- Freestyle clinics
- Indoor ski/snowboard treadmill sessions
- Skate jams
- Skatepark events
- Gear swaps
- Film nights
- Brand pop-ups
- Mountain festivals
- MTB park events
- BMX jams
- Community sessions

## Why This Can Work

The app has a clear wedge:

1. Start with ski and snowboard freestyle events.
2. Solve the fragmented information problem.
3. Add strong notification controls.
4. Add indoor training and off-season sports.
5. Add gear deals and marketplace monetization.
6. Become the local action-sport event layer.
