# 14 - Branding and Positioning

## Brand Direction

JamRadar should feel:

- Local
- Useful
- Sport-specific
- Clean
- Energetic
- Not corporate
- Not too childish
- Not too extreme-bro

The tone should be practical but alive.

## Possible Taglines

- Never miss the next local send.
- Events, jams, demos, and drops near you.
- Your radar for ski, snow, skate, and send culture.
- Find the next rail jam before your friend texts you too late.
- Local action-sport events without the feed chaos.
- Set your radius. Pick your events. Get the signal.

## Positioning Statement

JamRadar is the event alert app for ski, snowboard, skate, and action-sport communities. Users choose their radius and interests, then get notified about relevant events, indoor training sessions, gear deals, and local happenings without needing to follow dozens of scattered accounts.

## Differentiation

JamRadar is different from social media because it is:

- Location-based
- Event-first
- Filtered
- Calendar-friendly
- Notification-controlled
- Built for action sports

JamRadar is different from generic event apps because it understands:

- Rail jams
- Terrain parks
- Indoor ski/snowboard training
- Demo days
- Gear swaps
- Mountain culture
- Local shops
- Seasonal sports

## Visual Identity Ideas

Possible style:

- Dark mode first
- Snow/black/bright accent palette
- Map pins
- Poster-style event cards
- Mountain contour lines
- Trail map inspiration
- Clean icons for sport categories

## App Personality

The app should feel like a sharp local guide, not a bloated event directory.

Examples of notification copy:

- “New rail jam within 80 km.”
- “Registration closes tomorrow.”
- “Indoor snowboard session added this weekend.”
- “Three events near you this Saturday.”
- “Gear swap posted by a shop you follow.”
