# 03 - Target Users

## Primary User Groups

### 1. Skiers and Snowboarders

These users want to find:

- Rail jams
- Park events
- Demo days
- Banked slaloms
- Local competitions
- Freestyle clinics
- Gear swaps
- Indoor training sessions

They care about:

- Distance
- Date
- Cost
- Skill level
- Registration deadline
- Prize pool
- Whether friends are going

### 2. Terrain Park Riders

These users are the early adopter crowd.

They are likely to care about:

- Rail jams
- Night park sessions
- New park setups
- Best trick contests
- Sponsor events
- Park crew announcements
- Indoor rail/slope training

This group gives the app personality and credibility.

### 3. Mountains and Resorts

Mountains can use the app to post:

- Events
- Demo days
- Competitions
- Live music
- Clinics
- Family events
- Pass deadlines
- Park updates
- Season opener details
- Summer activities

They benefit from:

- More attendance
- Direct reach
- Analytics
- Targeted notifications
- Event visibility outside their own followers

### 4. Indoor Ski/Snowboard Training Facilities

These are a strong fit.

They can post:

- Open training times
- Freestyle treadmill sessions
- Beginner classes
- Private coaching slots
- Off-season programs
- Summer camps
- Pre-season tune-up sessions
- Package deals
- Dryland conditioning sessions

Indoor facilities help solve the seasonality problem.

### 5. Skateparks and Skate Shops

They can post:

- Skate jams
- Best trick contests
- Lessons
- Product drops
- Shop nights
- Film premieres
- Team rider demos
- Gear swaps

Skateboarding is a natural off-season extension because the culture overlaps with snowboard and freestyle communities.

### 6. Local Shops

Ski, snowboard, skate, and bike shops can post:

- Sales
- Demo nights
- Wax clinics
- Tuning clinics
- Gear swaps
- Team events
- Movie nights
- Product launches

They may become some of the first paying customers.

### 7. Brands

Brands can use JamRadar for:

- Sponsored event listings
- Demo tours
- Product drops
- Athlete appearances
- Regional campaigns
- Giveaways

### 8. Parents and Youth Riders

Parents want clean information:

- Age categories
- Safety rules
- Cost
- Waivers
- Start times
- Skill level
- Coaching options
- Spectator info

This user group may be more willing to pay for premium planning tools.
