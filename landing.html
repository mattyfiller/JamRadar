Install in 30 seconds.

JamRadar runs as a Progressive Web App — no App Store needed. Add it to your home screen and it works exactly like a native app, including offline.

iPhone

Safari

  1. Open the app in Safari (not Chrome)
  2. Tap the Share button (square + arrow)
  3. Scroll to Add to Home Screen
  4. Tap Add
Android

Chrome

  1. Open the app in Chrome
  2. You'll see Install JamRadar? banner
  3. Or menu → Install app
  4. Tap and you're done
Windows / Mac

Edge or Chrome

  1. Open in Edge or Chrome
  2. Click the install icon in the address bar
  3. Or menu → Install JamRadar
  4. Get a taskbar / Dock icon

FAQ

Quick answers. Got more questions? Email me.

Is this free?

Yes — completely free during beta. We may add premium features later for organizers (featured listings, analytics) but rider browsing will always be free.

Why do I need to set my city and radius?

So we only show you events you can actually get to. No wasted notifications about a rail jam in a different country.

Where do the events come from?

Right now: hand-curated weekly from major mountains, indoor facilities, and shops in our launch region. Soon: an automated ingest pipeline pulling from Eventbrite, iCal feeds, and resort calendars. Organizers can also post their events directly through the app.

Why not just check Instagram?

You already do — and you're missing things. JamRadar is the inbox for events you'd otherwise scroll past. Plus: no algorithm, no engagement bait, no feed fatigue. Just events near you.

Can I post my shop or facility's events?

Yes. After installing, go to Profile → Switch to organizer mode. You'll get a dashboard for posting, editing, and tracking analytics on your events. Verified-organizer status is free during pilot — DM us once your first event is live.

What about used gear / a marketplace?

Coming in Phase 2. Right now JamRadar handles new gear deals from shops and brands; peer-to-peer used gear sales are next on the roadmap once we have a critical mass of riders. Tell us if you'd use it.

Why not in the App Store / Play Store?

We're starting as a PWA — installs from the browser, works on every platform with one codebase. Native store listings are coming once the beta is solid. The good news: PWAs feel and work like native apps — push notifications, offline, home screen icon, fullscreen.

Where do you operate?

North America first. Ontario, Quebec, and the Banff trio (Sunshine, Lake Louise, Norquay) are seeded for launch. Other regions come online as riders sign up there.
