# JamRadar — Outreach Templates

Copy-paste-and-adapt scripts for the first 4 weeks. Replace `[URL]` with your live Netlify URL throughout.

---

## 1. Beta launch text — to friends + riding crew

Send via iMessage / WhatsApp / Telegram, one-on-one (not a group blast):

```
Hey — built a thing. JamRadar finds local rail jams, mountain events, indoor sessions, skate jams, and gear deals near you. Toronto-focused right now.

Install: [URL]
On iPhone: Safari → Share → Add to Home Screen → tap the icon.
On Android: Chrome → menu → Install app.

Tell me anything that's broken, missing, or weird. No wrong answers.
```

**Don't** send this to a group chat. People feel social pressure not to engage. One-on-one gets real feedback.

---

## 2. Shop / brand outreach — Instagram DM

To local ski/snowboard/skate shops, brands, indoor facilities. Personalize the first line:

```
Hey [shop name] — love your demo days. I built a free events app called JamRadar that helps local riders find rail jams, mountain events, indoor sessions, and gear deals. Thought it'd be a good fit for the [Toronto / Ontario / wherever] scene.

Two ways it could help you:
1) You post your events through it. Riders following [your shop / your sport / your area] get a notification automatically.
2) Your in-store gear deals get listed alongside affiliate deals from bigger sites.

It's free. No catch. I'm trying to seed real events into the app before opening it up wider — would you want to be one of the first orgs on it?

Link to install: [URL]
```

Don't send to more than 5 shops in your first week. You can't keep up with replies otherwise.

## 3. Mountain park crew / event organizer cold outreach — Email or DM

For Blue Mountain Park Crew, Mont Tremblant, Tier-1 mountains. More formal:

**Subject**: Your park events on JamRadar — free pilot

```
Hi [name or "[mountain] team"],

I'm building JamRadar, a local-first events app for skiers, snowboarders, and skaters. Riders set their location, sports, and notification preferences and only get pinged about events they actually care about — no scattered Instagram following dozens of accounts.

I'm seeding the [Ontario / Quebec / wherever] region first. [Mountain]'s rail jams, comps, and demo days are exactly the kind of high-signal events I want featured.

The pitch for you:
• Free verified-organizer badge during pilot
• Direct posting (no email-list-then-wait flow)
• Push notifications to riders within your radius the moment events drop
• Analytics: views, saves, registration clicks

Would a 15-minute call work? Happy to walk you through it. Otherwise the install link is below — feel free to poke around as a rider first.

[URL]

Thanks,
[Your name]
```

Send to: marketing/events email at the resort, or DM the resort's IG. Send no more than 3/week — mountains move slowly.

## 4. Soft launch — Instagram / TikTok caption

For when you make a 30-second screen recording of the app in use. Post it once, after week 2, when you have ~10 active beta riders giving you good feedback:

```
JamRadar 📡
local action-sport radar
rail jams · mountain events · indoor training · skate jams · gear deals

set your radius, pick your sports, only get pinged about events you'd actually go to.

free during beta — link in bio
```

Hashtags: `#snowboarding #skiing #skateboarding #toronto #freestyle #raildjam #ridingseason` (adapt to your launch region).

## 5. Subreddit post — `/r/snowboarding` or `/r/skiing` (regional sub better)

Don't post to the big subs day one. Find your **regional** sub:
- `/r/Toronto` (off-topic — only post if you're meeting their rules)
- `/r/snowboardingNB`, `/r/snowboardOntario`, `/r/snowboardingontario` (regional)
- `/r/skateboarding` (huge — wait until you have 50+ active riders)

Title:
```
[BETA] Built a local events app for [region] riders — looking for testers
```

Body:
```
Hey [region] riders,

I built JamRadar — a free app that finds local rail jams, mountain events, indoor training sessions, skate jams, and gear deals based on where you ride and what you ride. Only sends you notifications about events that match your sports and radius.

It's mobile-first (PWA, no app store yet), running in beta. Looking for ~10 testers in [region] who'd give honest feedback.

Install: [URL] (Safari → Share → Add to Home Screen on iPhone; Chrome → Install app on Android)

What's seeded so far:
- 30+ events across [region] this season
- Blue Mountain, Tremblant, Banff trio, indoor training facilities, Toronto skate parks

What I want feedback on:
- Are the events you'd actually want to know about?
- Notifications — too many? not enough? right ones?
- Anything broken or weird

Drop a comment or DM me. Happy to add events you know about.
```

## 6. Reply templates for common feedback

**"It's missing X event"** →
> Adding it now — give me 10 minutes. Thanks for the heads-up. Anything else missing in [their area]?

**"I'm not getting any events near me"** →
> What's your home city + radius set to? Also DM me a screenshot of your prefs. The seed list is currently Ontario/Quebec/Banff-heavy; we'll expand based on where the testers are.

**"The map is wrong"** →
> What specifically? Map's a real Leaflet integration — should show real geography. Send a screenshot if you can.

**"How do I sell my old skis on this?"** →
> Marketplace is Phase 2 — coming once we have ~100 active riders. For now, gear deals tab is shop/brand only. Noted you'd use it though.

**"I'd want to post events from my shop"** →
> Profile → Switch to organizer mode → post events. Send me your shop name and I'll add the verified badge once a couple are live.

**"I forgot it was on my phone"** →
> Fair. What would have reminded you — a push notification, an email, a friend mentioning an event? Trying to figure out how the loop should work.

---

## 7. Anti-patterns

- **Don't post the install link to LinkedIn.** Wrong audience.
- **Don't make a Product Hunt launch.** You're not ready for it. Save it for when you have 100+ active users and actual differentiation.
- **Don't message strangers cold on IG who haven't liked anything riding-related.** It's spam.
- **Don't ask for feedback in a group chat.** People stay silent in groups.
- **Don't tweet "I built an app, RT plz"** at 2am with no context. Show, don't tell.
- **Don't run paid ads yet.** You'll burn money on people who'll churn. Earn the first 100 users one-by-one.

---

## 8. The single most useful 3-line message

If you read nothing else above, send this to 10 people **today**:

```
Built a local events app — rail jams, mountain events, indoor training, skate jams, gear deals.
[URL]
Tell me what's broken.
```

Direct. No hype. Invites feedback. That's the whole strategy for the first 30 days.
